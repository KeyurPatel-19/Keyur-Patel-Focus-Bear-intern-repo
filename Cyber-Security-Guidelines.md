# Cyber Security

## Research & Learn

### What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?

Common cyber security threats in a remote work environment include phishing emails, suspicious links, weak passwords, unsecured Wi-Fi networks, and unauthorized access to accounts. These threats can allow attackers to steal sensitive information or gain access to company systems.

### What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?

Few best practices include using strong and unique passwords, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), keeping software updated, avoiding suspicious links or downloads, and using secure internet connections when accessing work accounts.

### Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?

Locking your computer prevents other people from accessing your files, accounts, or sensitive company information while you are away. This helps protect both personal and company data.

### How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?

If I receive a suspicious email or link, I will avoid clicking on it and check the sender carefully. If it looks like a phishing attempt, I will delete the message or report it instead of interacting with it.

### What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?

A strong password usually includes a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. A password manager helps store passwords securely and allows me to use different passwords for different accounts without needing to remember them all.

### Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring another verification step when logging in. Even if someone gets the password, they cannot access the account without the second verification. It should be enabled on all important accounts whenever possible.

## Reflection

### What security measures do you currently follow, and where can you improve?

I currently use strong passwords and try to be careful when opening emails or clicking links. However, I can improve by enabling two-factor authentication on more accounts and using a password manager to manage my passwords securely.

### How can you make secure behaviour a habit rather than an afterthought?

I can make secure behaviour a habit by always checking links before clicking them, locking my computer when I step away, and regularly reviewing my account security settings.

### What steps will you take to ensure your passwords and accounts are secure?

I will use strong and unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication where possible, avoid sharing my login details, and store my passwords securely using a password manager.

### What would you do if you suspected a security breach or suspicious activity on your account?

If I suspect a security issue, I would immediately change my password, secure my account, and report the issue to the appropriate team or supervisor so it can be investigated quickly.
