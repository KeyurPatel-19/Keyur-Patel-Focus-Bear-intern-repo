# Creating Your "User Manual for Me"

## Research & Learn

### What is a “User Manual for Me”, and why do teams use them?

A User Manual for Me is a short guide where a person explains how they like to work, communicate, and collaborate with others. Teams use it so that everyone understands each other’s working style, which helps avoid misunderstandings and makes teamwork smoother and more comfortable.

### What categories are helpful to include? (e.g., communication style, work habits, feedback preferences)

Some helpful categories to include are:

- Communication style
- Work habits
- Focus and productivity preferences
- Collaboration and teamwork style
- Feedback preferences
- Personal quirks or things teammates should know
  These sections help teammates understand how to work with someone effectively.

### How can writing a personal user manual improve teamwork and reduce misunderstandings?

Writing a personal user manual helps teammates understand each other’s expectations and working styles. It makes communication clearer and helps people respect different preferences. Because of this, teams can collaborate more easily and avoid confusion or misunderstandings.

## Reflection

### What do you need from teammates to do your best work? (e.g., direct communication, flexibility, detailed instructions, minimal interruptions)

To do my best work, I need clear instructions, good communication, and mutual respect. I also appreciate when teammates are supportive and open to discussing ideas. Having clear goals and deadlines helps me stay focused and organized.

### How do you prefer to communicate? (e.g., async messages, video calls, quick check-ins, long discussions)

I prefer clear and simple communication. For regular communication, I am comfortable with async messages like email. For discussions or problem solving, short meetings or video calls also work well.

### What helps you stay focused and productive? (e.g., quiet workspace, deep work sessions, structured to-do lists, music)

I stay focused when I have a clear task list and a quiet environment. Working on one task at a time helps me stay productive. Having a structured plan or to-do list also helps me manage my time better.

### How do you like to give and receive feedback? (e.g., clear & direct, with positive reinforcement, written vs. verbal feedback)

I prefer feedback that is clear, respectful, and constructive. I appreciate when feedback explains what went well and what can be improved. I also like feedback that includes examples so I can learn and improve.

### What are some quirks or things to know about your work style? (e.g., need time to process ideas, like to brainstorm out loud, prefer written instructions)

One thing to know about my work style is that I sometimes take a little time to think before responding, especially during discussions. If I am quiet in meetings, it usually means I am listening carefully and thinking about the topic.
