### What types of data are considered confidential at Focus Bear?

At Focus Bear, confidential data includes any information that should be protected and not shared carelessly. Based on the privacy policy, this includes:
-Personal identification data like email address and phone number
-Habit and lifestyle data entered into the app
-Academic or professional background shared in surveys
-Marketing preferences and contact details
-Technical data such as device type, operating system, and error logs
-Payment and subscription-related information
-Feedback, survey responses, and purchase history
-Sensitive data such as health-related details or religious clues that may appear in habits or optional surveys
-Internal company information, systems, and service-related data
This data is confidential because it belongs to users or the company and must be handled carefully.

### What are best practices for handling confidential data?

Some best practices for handling confidential data are:
-Only access the information you need for your work
-Do not share private data with people who are not authorized to see it
-Use secure platforms and approved tools when storing or sharing information
-Keep passwords strong and use two-factor authentication
-Be careful when sending emails, files, or messages so they do not go to the wrong person
-Avoid downloading or saving sensitive information on personal devices unless absolutely necessary
-Never discuss confidential information in public or casual spaces
-Log out of systems when finished and keep devices secure
-Follow company privacy policies and security procedures at all times
These habits help reduce the chance of mistakes or data leaks.

### How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?

If I suspect a data breach or accidentally share confidential information, I should act quickly and honestly. I should, Report it immediately to my supervisor or the right person in the company, Clearly explain what happened, Share what type of information may have been exposed, Avoid hiding the mistake or delaying the report, Follow the instructions given to help reduce further risk. The most important thing is to speak up quickly so the company can take the right action as soon as possible.

## Reflection

### What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?

To handle data securely in my daily tasks, I can:
-Only use data that is necessary for the task I am doing
-Double-check before sharing files, messages, or screenshots
-Use strong passwords and keep my accounts secure
-Avoid saving sensitive information in unsafe places
-Be careful when working on personal devices
-Respect company rules about privacy and confidentiality
-Report any suspicious activity or accidental mistake immediately
These small daily habits can make a big difference in keeping data safe.

### How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?

Sensitive information should be handled carefully at every stage.

Store :
It should be stored only in secure and approved systems, not in random folders, personal notes, or unsafe apps.

Share :
It should only be shared with authorized people and through proper company channels. Before sending anything, I should check that the right file is going to the right person.

Dispose :
If sensitive information is no longer needed, it should be deleted safely and not kept as an unnecessary extra copy. Printed copies should also be disposed of securely if used.

The main idea is to keep access limited and avoid leaving sensitive data exposed.

### What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?

Some common mistakes include:
-Sending information to the wrong person
-Using weak passwords
-Sharing confidential data in public chats or unsafe platforms
-Leaving devices unlocked
-Saving private files carelessly on personal devices
-Collecting more information than needed
-Ignoring company privacy rules
-Not reporting mistakes quickly
These mistakes can be avoided by slowing down, checking carefully before sharing anything, using secure systems, and following privacy practices consistently.
