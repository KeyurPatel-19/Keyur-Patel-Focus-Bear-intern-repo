## Research & Learn

### What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment? (Hint: Think about communication, meeting etiquette, and teamwork.)

Professional behaviour
  Communicating clearly and respectfully in messages and meetings
  Being punctual for meetings and completing tasks on time
  Listening to teammates and being supportive
  Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them

Unprofessional behaviour
  Ignoring messages or not responding to teammates
  Interrupting others during meetings
  Being disrespectful or blaming others for mistakes
  Not completing assigned tasks or missing deadlines

### What does respectful communication look like in remote teams? (e.g., tone in messages, handling disagreements, responding to feedback)

Respectful communication in remote teams means using clear, polite, and supportive language when messaging or speaking with teammates. It also means listening carefully, giving others time to speak, and responding thoughtfully. If there is disagreement, it should be handled calmly and professionally by discussing ideas rather than criticizing people.

### How can you give and receive feedback professionally? (e.g., focus on actions, not people; assume good intent; ask for clarification instead of reacting defensively)

When receiving feedback, it is helpful to listen carefully, stay open-minded, and see it as an opportunity to improve. Asking questions for clarification and thanking the person for their feedback also shows professionalism.

## Reflection

### Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?

During my university projects and internship work, I have seen that good teamwork happens when everyone communicates clearly, respects each other's ideas, and shares responsibilities fairly. Regular updates, supportive communication, and helping each other when someone is stuck make the team more productive and positive.

### What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

-Write messages clearly and politely
-Listen carefully to others' opinions
-Avoid assumptions and ask questions when unsure
-Respect different ideas, backgrounds, and communication styles

### How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?

I can help create a positive environment by being supportive, respectful, and cooperative with my teammates. I can also stay responsible with my tasks, communicate openly, and maintain a friendly attitude during collaboration.

### How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?

-Review the meeting agenda or topic beforehand
-Prepare questions or updates about my work
-Be on time and ready to participate
-Take notes so I remember important points and action items

### What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?

Being proactive means taking initiative instead of waiting to be told what to do. For example, asking questions when something is unclear, suggesting ideas, updating the team about progress, and looking for ways to improve the work.

### How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?

First, I would send a polite reminder message or follow-up email asking if they had time to review or respond. If there is still no response after some time, I would check again respectfully or mention it in the next meeting. Escalation should only happen when the task is urgent and necessary for progress.

### What are some things you won't do?

-I will not communicate disrespectfully with teammates
-I will not ignore responsibilities or deadlines
-I will not blame others for mistakes
-I will not create negativity or conflict in the team
